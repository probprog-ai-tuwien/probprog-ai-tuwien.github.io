Prerequisities

We expect that you have working knowledge of Python and are familiar with Jupyter Notebooks.
We also require basic knowledge of probability theory and statistics. This course will feature a lot of mathematics for expository reasons, altough we do not require you to do any mathematical manipulations in the exercises.

Lecture Mode

The six lectures will be held in-person in Seminarraum FAV 01 A (Seminarraum 183/2).
The assignment discussion session are online via Zoom and attendance is requored. We will compare solutions and you can earn bonus points.
Tutor office ours are in online via Zoom as well.
The final project presentations will be mainly in-person. Exceptions can be made for those who cannot attend in-person who will present via Zoom.

Assignments

In addition to the lecture, you will implement a minimal probabilistic programming language in individual assignments (A1 to A4):
  • Assignment 1 (A1) - Introduction to PPL (10 points): This will be your first hands-on experience with probabilistic programming.
  • Assignment 2 (A2) - Minimal PPL + Likelihood Weighting (15 points): You learn how to implement the core of a PPL in Python and write your first inference algorithm.
  • Assignment 3 (A3) - Metropolis Hastings (15 points): You implement the general-purpose Metropolis Hastings inference algorithm
  • Assignment 4 (A4) - HMC and ADVI (25 points): You implement the state-of-the-art inference algorithms HMC and ADVI

Group Project

In groups of up to 5 students, you will propose and work on a final project. There will be a presentation session at the end of the semester, where each group is expected to present for 20 minutes and answer questions.
Projects may be anything related to probabilistic programming.
For instance,
  • Applying probabilistic programming to a non-trivial problem.
  • Implementing an advanced inference algorithm in our minimal PPL.
  • Implementing a PPL following a different design principle.
  • Benchmarking inference algorithms on a benchmark problem set.
We will give more project ideas during the lectures.

Grading

Your grade will be a combination of assignments scores and final project score.
  • 40% Assignments
  • 60% Project

Grading Scale

The points of the theoretical and practical part sum to exactly 100 points. The points map to grades as follows:
  • S1: 88-100
  • U2: 75-87.99
  • B3: 63-74.99
  • G4: 50-62.99
  • N5: 0-49.99

Academic Honesty

We expect all students to work on their own (both for the practical assignments and the tests). Any kind of plagiarism will result in expulsion of the course with a grade N5 (Nicht Genügend).
